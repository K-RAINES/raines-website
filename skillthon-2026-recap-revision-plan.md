# Skillthon 2026 Recap Editorial Review

Inspected: live page at `https://rainesusa.org/skillthon-2026-recap.html` and local `skillthon-2026-recap.html`.

## Overall Findings

The page has useful recap material: date, venue, attendance counts, photos, project names, awards imagery, testimonials, and a slide link. The main editorial issue is that several passages still read like internal content-production notes about photo placement, caption accuracy, and revision history rather than public-facing event copy. The recap also needs a clearer results section: first place is identified, but second place, third place, special recognition criteria, judges, mentors, prize details, and demo links are either missing or only implied by photos. Several project blurbs use generic AI/product language and should be revised with concrete details from the actual demos.

No obvious literal placeholders such as `[insert date]` or `more details to follow` were visible, but multiple passages function as unfinished placeholders because they reference information the page does not provide.

Where an English item has a Korean counterpart on the page, revise the Korean copy in parallel rather than translating the current draft literally.

## Story 01 - From Slides To A Live Demo

1. Original: "One participant arrived about an hour late with family and without prior Codex experience."
   - Problem: Personal/unflattering detail without naming the participant, project, or relevance to the final result.
   - Proposed revision: "A participant who started with slides and no Codex experience turned [project name] into a working demo by first converting the idea into a product spec." Information needed: project name, whether the participant should remain anonymous, and whether the late arrival/family detail is approved for publication.

2. Original: "The turning point was not a single magic prompt; it was converting the slide idea into a product specification that Codex could act on."
   - Problem: Useful point, but "single magic prompt" is a familiar AI cliche.
   - Proposed revision: "The breakthrough came when the slide concept became a clear product specification that Codex could execute."

3. Original: "The photo support for this story is kept general: project presentation scenes and a visible software demo. That is more accurate than attaching the anecdote to a photo that does not clearly identify the same participant or product."
   - Problem: Internal photo-risk note; it tells readers the evidence may not match the story. There is no verified photo of this particular participant/project for Story 01.
   - Proposed revision: Remove from public copy. Do not publish "photo not available," missing-photo, or evidence-gap wording. Either keep the story text-only or use neutral event-level images with captions that describe only what is visibly shown, without implying they depict this participant or product.

4. Original: "Precise takeaway: agentic AI helped convert rough ideas into visible prototypes by turning intent into a clearer, buildable brief."
   - Problem: "Precise takeaway" sounds self-conscious, and the sentence still reads generic.
   - Proposed revision: "Takeaway: the strongest progress came when builders translated intent into a product spec the agent could follow."

## Story 02 - Domain Expertise Won

1. Original: "The first-place story was about a participant using their own field experience to make a practical workflow, after getting over the initial setup and interface barrier."
   - Problem: Vague result and vague obstacle; the winner and exact demo outcome are not named in the story text.
   - Proposed revision: "First place went to Preaching Sermon Creator, a demo that helped pastors generate sermon ideas, organize structure, and draft sermon materials." Do not name the individual builder in public copy. Information needed: what the live demo actually showed.

2. Original: "The important point is not that every builder started from a conventional coding background."
   - Problem: Defensive phrasing argues against a claim readers have not seen.
   - Proposed revision: "The result highlighted the Skillthon's premise: domain expertise can become a working prototype when the builder can clearly specify the problem."

## Story 03 - Constraints Became Part Of The Story

1. Original: "Another participant faced a practical blocker: setup constraints reduced the usable build time, but the team still reached a live demo and placed second."
   - Problem: "Team" is inaccurate; this was one participant. The project and setup constraint should be specific without naming the participant or describing time lost.
   - Proposed revision: "One participant built Researcher Matching from scratch in about two hours while using a borrowed laptop, then reached a live demo and placed second."

2. Original: "Second place came after a focused build under real time and setup constraints."
   - Problem: Caption is broadly accurate, but it can be more specific without naming the participant.
   - Proposed revision: "Second place came after the participant built Researcher Matching from scratch in about two hours on a borrowed laptop."

## Story 04 - Smaller Made It Better

1. Original: "Smaller made it better"
   - Problem: Sounds like spin around attendance rather than a recap claim supported by facts.
   - Proposed revision: "A smaller group of demo builders made room for focused feedback."

2. Original: "Fourteen people registered, four original registrants attended on site, two organizers joined as participants, and one student joined late after passing by."
   - Problem: Useful facts, but "original registrants" and "student joined late after passing by" are informal and may raise privacy/context questions.
   - Proposed revision: "Fourteen people registered; seven presented live, including four registered attendees, two organizers, and one walk-in participant."

3. Original: "The final count of seven presenters created a better presentation hour than a packed schedule might have allowed."
   - Problem: Speculative and defensive.
   - Proposed revision: "With seven presenters, each demo had time for explanation, feedback, and questions."

## Working Demos And Project Ideas

1. Original: "The demos were rooted in real interests: ministry, pet care, researcher matching, patent search, startup evaluation, immersive K-pop viewing, and news/product prototyping."
   - Problem: "news/product prototyping" is referenced here but no corresponding project card appears below.
   - Proposed revision: Add a project card for the news/product prototype, or remove that phrase from the list.

2. Original: "Recognition"
   - Problem: Unclear whether this means second place, special recognition, honorable mention, or a non-ranked award.
   - Proposed revision: Use a precise award label such as "Special Recognition," "Honorable Mention," or the actual placement.

3. Original: "A concept to connect researchers, technical experts, and collaborators around high-fit R&D and innovation needs."
   - Problem: Generic product phrasing; no concrete user, workflow, or demo behavior.
   - Proposed revision: "Researcher Matching helped [target users] find [type of collaborators] by matching [inputs/signals]." Information needed: demo inputs, matching logic, and intended users.

4. Original: "An agent concept to scout patent activity and surface technology signals, commercialization opportunities, and relevant IP."
   - Problem: Buzzword-heavy and broad.
   - Proposed revision: "Patent Scout Agent searched [patent/source type] for [technology area] and summarized [specific output]." Information needed: what the demo searched and what it returned.

5. Original: "A dashboard concept for comparing signals and identifying high-potential ventures in a more structured way."
   - Problem: Vague; "signals" and "more structured way" do not tell readers what was built.
   - Proposed revision: "Startup Evaluation Dashboard compared [specific metrics] across startups to help users identify [decision/use case]." Information needed: metrics and demo workflow.

6. Original: "IP / TIPA"
   - Problem: Acronym is undefined for general readers.
   - Proposed revision: Spell out TIPA on first use or change the tag to a reader-facing category such as "Patent Search."

7. Original: "An AI-powered companion concept to keep pets engaged, stimulated, and comforted when owners are away."
   - Problem: Polished but generic; could describe almost any pet-tech idea.
   - Proposed revision: "Your Pet's Second Best Friend used [input/device/content type] to [specific pet-care action] while owners were away." Information needed: actual feature shown in the demo.

## Participant Quotes

1. Original: "These lines come from the reviewed testimonial document, using names only where public permission was given and anonymous wording where requested."
   - Problem: Internal provenance note; readers do not know what the "reviewed testimonial document" is.
   - Proposed revision: "Participants described the day as a practical introduction to building with AI agents, from first prompts to working demos."

2. Original: "Better prompts and better structure lead to better output."
   - Problem: In the Korean testimonial section, this remains in English without explanation.
   - Proposed revision: Translate it for the Korean page, or label it as an original English quote. Example Korean revision: "더 나은 프롬프트와 구조가 더 나은 결과로 이어진다는 점이 가장 큰 배움이었습니다."

3. Original: "dopamine hit"
   - Problem: Memorable, but highly informal; confirm the speaker approved this exact public wording.
   - Proposed revision: Keep only if it is an approved direct quote. Otherwise paraphrase: "after the first hurdle, each step felt unexpectedly energizing."

3. Original: "Third-place recognition during the awards segment."

## Event Materials

1. Original: "For readers who want the framework behind the event, the intro deck explains the move from vibe coding to agentic engineering."
   - Problem: "Vibe coding" and "agentic engineering" are insider terms without a plain-language bridge.
   - Proposed revision: "The intro deck explains the Skillthon workflow: using AI agents to turn rough ideas into specs, prototypes, reviews, and demos."

2. Original: "the live intro deck shared in the Skillthon channel."
   - Problem: "Skillthon channel" assumes context and does not tell public readers where it was shared.
   - Proposed revision: "the intro deck shared with participants during the event" or specify the channel if it is public.

## Closing / Call To Action

1. Original: "K-RAINES will continue creating practical spaces where Korean and Korea-connected professionals can learn, build, and collaborate across AI, robotics, healthcare, biotech, and deep tech."
   - Problem: Generic organizational boilerplate; no next step for interested readers.
   - Proposed revision: "K-RAINES will continue hosting hands-on programs for Korean and Korea-connected professionals in AI, robotics, healthcare, biotech, and deep tech. Follow [channel/link] for the next workshop or demo day." Information needed: preferred contact, mailing list, event page, or social link.

## Information Needed To Complete The Recap

- Full award results: first, second, third, special recognition/honorable mention labels, project names, builder/team names, and prize amounts if public.
- Judging criteria and judge/mentor names if public.
- Demo links, GitHub links, slide links, or screenshots for each project if available.
- Whether personal details about late arrival, family attendance, setup issues, borrowed computer, and walk-in participation are approved for publication.
- The missing "news/product prototyping" project details, or confirmation that it should be removed.
- Definitions for Codex, TIPA, vibe coding, and agentic engineering for readers outside the event.
- Korean translation policy: direct translation of English recap, localized Korean recap tone, or shorter Korean summary.
